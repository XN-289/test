# reOpenTest TONE Standards (The Transparent Engineer)

## Voice Characteristics
- **Tone**: Pragmatic, rigorously evidence-based, confidently understated, and unapologetically professional. We speak like an engineer explaining a blueprint, not a salesperson closing a deal.
- **Diction**: Clinical, precise, and structural. Use terms like "verifiable reliability," "workflow integration," "batch consistency," and "confidence intervals." Never use obfuscation or marketing fluff.
- **Attitude**: Obsessed with the "Glass Factory" principle. We view the "Black Box Risk" of the industry as our primary enemy. We believe transparency is the shortest path to ultimate trust. We do not ask for blind faith; we demand verification.

## Audience Modulation (The Rendering Engine)
The core persona ("The Transparent Engineer") remains constant, but the vocabulary and focal points must adapt dynamically based on the target audience:

- **1. B2B Dealers / Procurement (The Reliable Partner)**
  - **Focus**: Commercial reliability, compliance, and supply chain stability.
  - **Diction**: `stable supply`, `regulatory compliance`, `turnkey procurement`, `batch-to-batch consistency`.
  - **Tone**: Pragmatic, bottom-line oriented. Show them why partnering with us removes their business risks.

- **2. Professional Users - Doctors/Lab Techs (The Rigorous Colleague)**
  - **Focus**: Clinical efficiency, accuracy, and workflow integration.
  - **Diction**: `workflow optimization`, `sensitivity/specificity`, `clinical evidence`, `actionable results`.
  - **Tone**: Peer-to-peer, clinical, respectful of their time. Cut the preamble and go straight to the performance metrics. 

- **3. DTC Consumers (The Clear Health Guide)**
  - **Focus**: Peace of mind through clarity, ease of use, and privacy.
  - **Diction**: `clear results`, `simple 3-step process`, `privacy protected`, `next actionable steps`.
  - **Tone**: Direct, reassuring but *not* overly emotional. We comfort them by removing ambiguity, not by making empty emotional promises.

- **4. Strategic Partners - OEM/ODM (The Confident CTO)**
  - **Focus**: Deep manufacturing capabilities, platform strength, and long-term vision.
  - **Diction**: `NomoFlow™`, `predictive quality control`, `source manufacturing`, `platform integration`.
  - **Tone**: Visionary but grounded in industrial reality. Emphasize our backend superiority and algorithmic control.

## Prohibited Phrases
- "Revolutionary breakthrough..." (We don't rely on hype; we rely on clinical data.)
- "The ultimate/perfect solution..." (We provide precise components to optimize specific workflows, not magic pills.)
- "Feel safe and secure..." (We do not discuss *feelings*. We present *evidence*.)
- "Industry-leading..." or "Best-in-class..." (Unless immediately followed by the exact comparative data and its source.)
- "Effortless..." or "Like magic..." (Diagnostic science is hard, rigorous work. We respect the complexity by making it clear, not by pretending it's easy.)

## Style Constraints
- **Sentences**: Clear, direct, and factual. Focus on *how* we achieve the result, not just declaring that we do. (e.g., "Our data shows..." instead of "We are the best...").
- **Data Isolation**: Treat numbers with absolute reverence. Specifications, percentages (e.g., *99.8%*), and batch numbers must be precise and visually distinct (always implying a monospaced, immutable nature).
- **The "Evidence Anchor"**: Every major claim or performance metric *must* be accompanied by a direct call-to-action for verification (e.g., `[View Clinical Report]`, `[Download IFU]`).
- **Formatting**: Use Markdown structurally. Bold for core concepts (like **`NomoFlow™`**), strict bullet points for workflows, and distinct separation between *Logic* (the explanation) and *Proof* (the data).
